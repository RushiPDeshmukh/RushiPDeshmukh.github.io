About me

Welcome! I’m Rushi, a Robotics Engineer eager to drive innovation and make a positive difference.

Being a tinkerer at heart, automation has been a keen area of interest for me since childhood. Creating impactful innovations for a better world through automation is my lifelong dream and I have strived to achieve it to the best of my abilities
With a solid background in electronics, I possess an in-depth understanding of the interplay between low-level software and hardware, focusing on sensors and actuators. The process of creating automation solutions from the ground up is incredibly exhilarating for me.
While leading the Product Innovation lab as an undergraduate, I directed a fully funded project to create a biomedical arm for restoring sensation in paralyzed individuals. My journey at WPI as a research fellow in the MER lab enriched my research experience. Following this, I joined Lumasort, where I utilized and expanded my Reinforcement Learning expertise to automate the glass recycling industry
Armed with a Master’s in Robotics from Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) and industrial experience with Reinforcement learning, robots and SLAM, I am keen to bring my expertise to teams dedicated to creating tangible, real-world impact.

Projects

  • Reinforcement Learning

    Present

    Truncated Quantile Critics(TQC) based Pick and Place Agent for Panda Robot

    The project focused on developing and implementing a reinforcement learning (RL) agent for a pick-and-place task within the PyBullet Panda gym environment. The PyBullet Panda gym provides a robust simulation platform with a realistic physics engine, allowing for intricate manipulation tasks and extensive agent training. The TQC (Truncated Quantile Critics) algorithm was employed due to its superior performance in handling continuous action spaces and mitigating overestimation biases, which are often challenges in RL. The PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization) algorithm was also used for comparison, highlighting the significant advantages of TQC in this context. Further investigations are ongoing into integrating Hierarchical Reinforcement Learning (HRL) and curriculum-based learning to continue advancing the capabilities and adaptability of the RL agent.

    https://github.com/RushiPDeshmukh/Centralized_Motion_Planner_Project
    TQC, PPO, HRL, PyBullet, Panda Robot
    The TQC-based RL agent achieved a remarkable 98% accuracy in completing the pick-and-place task, demonstrating its effectiveness and reliability. In contrast, the PPO-based agent attained only 15% accuracy, underscoring the superiority of TQC for this specific application. Comprehensive performance analysis was carried out through hyperparameter optimization using Tree-structured Parzen Estimator sampling with Optuna, which enhanced the overall efficiency of the model.

  • Reinforcement Learning

    Self-Play Based Learning for Multi-Robot RL agents playing Soccer

    The project involved implementing the Unity ML Agents Kit to train soccer-playing agents using the PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization) algorithm. Unity ML Agents Kit provided a versatile platform for simulating soccer environments and training agents through reinforcement learning. The approach utilized self-play strategies, where RL agents were pitted against previous versions of themselves to enhance learning and improve strategic decision-making. Additionally, ELO scores were employed for posthumous credit assignment (POCA), which allowed for rewarding cooperative RL agents based on their individual performances.

    https://github.com/RushiPDeshmukh/Centralized_Motion_Planner_Project
    Unity MLAgents, Self-Play, ELO score, Cooperative Multi-Agent RL (MARL)
    After just 2 days of training, the soccer-playing agents achieved an impressive ELO score of 1300. This rapid advancement demonstrated the effectiveness of the PPO algorithm and the self-play approach in developing high-performing agents. The use of ELO scores for POCA further facilitated the optimization of agent performance by providing a nuanced evaluation of individual contributions and cooperative behaviors.

  • Reinforcement Learning

    Sept 2023

    Reinforcement Learning Based Behaviour Planners for Autonomous Vehicles

    The project centered around validating the effectiveness of a hierarchical reinforcement learning (HRL) based behavior planning model by testing a PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization) RL agent within the Highway Gym environment. Highway Gym is a comprehensive simulation platform designed for traffic scenarios, offering a controlled setting to evaluate autonomous driving behaviors. The approach involved segmenting behavior planning into meta-actions such as merging, braking, switching lanes, and following lead vehicles, leveraging the hierarchical reinforcement learning framework to manage complex driving strategies effectively.

    https://github.com/RushiPDeshmukh/Centralized_Motion_Planner_Project
    PPO, HRL, Auto Curriculum Generation, PyGym
    The PPO RL agent achieved an impressive 88% accuracy, demonstrating the robustness and effectiveness of the hierarchical reinforcement learning-based behavior planning model. Task selection was optimized based on the learning pace through Auto Curriculum Generation, which resulted in a 45% faster convergence rate compared to traditional random curriculum learning techniques. This optimization not only improved learning efficiency but also highlighted the advantages of HRL in accelerating the training process and enhancing agent performance in dynamic driving environments.

    Designed and deployed a centralized motion planning system for autonomous vehicles (AVs) using algorithms such as A* variants and D* lite. The system also includes a time-priority-based conflict resolution strategy that allows for efficient navigation and safe operation of multiple AVs in a coordinated manner. The centralized controller provides a path for each AV to reach the destination, handles all deadlocks, and helps avoid collisions.
