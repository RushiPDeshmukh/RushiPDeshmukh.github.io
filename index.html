About me

I am a Robotics Engineer working as the lead developer at Lumasort. I graduated with a Master's in Robotics from Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) in May 2023. Driven by a passion for using creative and sophisticated engineering solutions to tackle real-world problems, I am skilled in reinforcement learning, motion planning, and robot perception systems.

Currently, I am the lead developer at Lumasort. Previously, I have worked as an embedded systems developer at MedHigh Education Platform, developing educational biomedical devices and creating hardware APIs for the same. I have also gained valuable experience through research conducted in the Manipulations and Environmental Robotics Lab at WPI, where I have been building reinforcement learning agents for complex hybrid action space systems. In addition, I have served as a Product Innovations Lab Manager, where I was responsible for problem solving, bringing innovative approaches, and leading interdisciplinary engineering teams. With my diverse background and skill set, I am looking forward to making a meaningful impact in the field of robotics.

    Centralized Motion Planner for Autonomous Vehicles

    Designed and deployed a centralized motion planning system for autonomous vehicles (AVs) using algorithms such as A* variants and D* lite. This motion planner was implemented on a simulation environment built using ROS (Robotic Operating System) and Pygame. The system also includes a time-priority-based conflict resolution strategy that allows for efficient navigation and safe operation of multiple AVs in a coordinated manner. The centralized controller provides a path for each AV to reach the destination, handles all deadlocks, and helps avoid collisions.

Considering the Naive A* as baseline, a 29% performance improvement was observed for the Combinational Planner and a 26% performance improvement was observed for the Prioritized Safe Interval Planning.
